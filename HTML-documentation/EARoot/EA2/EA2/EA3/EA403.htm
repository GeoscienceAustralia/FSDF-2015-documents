-- Definition --<br /></p><p>A unit is defined as a logical entity (stand-alone or part of an hierarchy of entities) used across various themes and sub-themes/datasets. It describes the generic information and behavior shared across all entities of the data foundation, e.g., name/object identifier (e.g., using gml:name and gml:identifier) and hierarchical topology respectively. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>-- Definition --<br /></p><p>Generic administrative unit feature type.<br /></p><p>-- Note --<br /></p><p>This covers administrative unit types that are not modeled in a more specialized schema. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>In many cases these relate to a specific administrative or service function, as delegated by the relevant government unit. These include<br /></p><p>- catchment management authorities<br /></p><p>- school zones<br /></p><p>- police districts<br /></p><p>- fire and emergency service areas<br /></p><p>- cadastral units (county, district, parish, hundred, division)<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>In other cases, the unit, through formally gazetted, is primarily ceremonial or informative with few or no administrative functions, such as <br /></p><p>- city, metropolitan area<br /></p><p>- locality, suburb<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>Administrative units generally do not intersect or overlap for a particular function or service within the scope of the government unit that authorizes it. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>There is a relationship with the government unit that is responsible for delegating the specific function, and there may be relationships with other administrative units. <br /></p>
