<b>User story 3 - </b><b><u>Finding </u></b><b>authoritative and appropriately scaled data for analysis </b><br /></p><p><br /></p><p>BHP Bilton is  doing a desktop exercise  to find out if a proposed mine site is within a particular jurisdiction to:<br /></p><p>- determine appropriate planning regulations and constraints  (State/LGA interaction)<br /></p><p>- determine who has authority to grant mine licence (State interaction)<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>In BHP GIS environment, a user zooms into the proposed mine site location to determine if the site is in State/Territory A or B. The user determines  that the representation of the State Boundary data set she is using is too generalized a line. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>She therefore goes online to find a better representation.<br /></p><p>1. The user searches for State Boundaries in the NSII and finds x data sets returned.<br /></p><p>2. She filters out results based on custodian (looking for authoritative data sets)<br /></p><p>3. For an authoritative data source she discovers that representations at 1:1m and 1:50K are available. <br /></p><p>4. She selects the 25k version, checks the licence of the data to ensure it is appropriate for her use and downloads it.<br /></p>
